
$CeO_{2}$ is the fully oxidized endpoint of the cerium oxide series. Has the simplest structure and the simplest chemistry.

**The fluorite structure (Fm-3m)**
$CeO_{2}$ adopts the fluorite crystal structure, one of the most common and well-studied oxide structures. The key geometric feature is the FCC cerium sublattice with oxygen filling all tetrahedral holes:
- Every $\mathrm{Ce}^{4+}$ is coordinated by 8 oxygens in a perfect cube (cubic coordination)
- Every $\mathrm{O}^{2-}$ - sits in a tetrahedron of $4\mathrm{Ce}^{4+}$ (tetrahedral coordination)
- The structure is fully cubic - maximum possible symmetry for an oxide

High symmetry has important consequences for calculations: $CeO_{2}$ is as simple as an oxide gets.
1. there are no low-symmetry distortions
2. no Jahn-Teller effects
3. no symmetry-breaking charge ordering
