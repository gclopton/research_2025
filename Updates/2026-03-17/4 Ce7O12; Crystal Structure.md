

$Ce_{7}O_{12}$ (mp-2629) is the intermediate oxide in the cerium oxide series - it sits between the fully oxidized CeO 2 and fully reduced $\mathrm{Ce2O3}$. It is not a simple compound: it is a vacancy-ordered superstructure derived from the CeO 2 fluorite parent. Starting from the CeO 2 fluorite framework and removing 2 oxygens per Ce 7014 formula unit in an ordered pattern produces the Ce7012 superstructure. This means Ce7O12 inherits the fluorite topology but with **ordered oxygen vacancies** that break the cubic symmetry down to rhombohedral.

| Property               | Value                                                                                         |
| :--------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Space group            | R-3 (No. 148) - rhombohedral                                                                  |
| Materials Project ID   | mp-2629                                                                                       |
| Lattice parameters     | $\|\mathrm{a}\|=\|\mathrm{b}\|=10.341 \ Å,\|\mathrm{c}\|=9.576 \ Å$                           |
| Angle between a and b  | $120^{\circ}$ (hexagonal setting)                                                             |
| Formula units per cell | $3 \times$ Ce7O12                                                                             |
| Atoms per cell         | $\mathbf{2 1 ~ C e} \boldsymbol{+} \mathbf{3 6} \mathbf{O} \boldsymbol{=} \mathbf{5 7}$ atoms |
| Cell volume            | $886.8 \ Å^3$ (295.6 $\ Å^3$ per formula unit)                                                |

**The two Ce Wyckoff sites:** - The 21 Ce atoms in the supercell occupy two distinct symmetry sites:

| Site | Wyckoff | Atoms | Count | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :---: |
| Triplet | 18 f | $1-6,8-13,15-20$ | 18 | Low-symmetry, grouped in triplets of 3 around each singlet |
| Singlet | $3 a$ | $7,14,21$ | 3 | High-symmetry, one per formula unit at $z=0,1 / 3,2 / 3$ |

This distinction is physically critical. The singlet 3a sites sit at special positions along the c -axis with higher local symmetry. The triplet 18 f sites cluster in layers around each singlet. As we discovered from our DFT calculations, the SCF independently placed $\mathbf{C e 4}+$ on all three singlet sites - the high-symmetry positions prefer the empty f -shell. The triplet sites carry the $\mathrm{Ce} 3+$ character.



**Ce–O bond lengths:** The Ce–O bond lengths reflect the mixed-valence character of the material. For a Ce3+ triplet site (atom 1):

|         Bond         |   Length $(\mathbf{Å})$    |
| :------------------: | :------------------------: |
| Ce3+-O (nearest, ×3) |     $2.295-2.347 \ Å$      |
| Mean nearest Ce3+-O  | $\mathbf{2 . 3 9 8 ~ \ Å}$ |

The physical structure of $Ce_{7}O_{12}$ is already more complex than $CeO_{2}$ and $Ce_{2}O_{3}$ in three ways:
1. Lower symmetry - R-3 vs Fm-3m ($CeO_{2}$) or the $La_{2}O_{3}$-type structure of $Ce_{2}O_{3}$
2. Two distinct Ce sites - triplets and singlets, with different chemical environments and different preferred charge states
3. 57 atoms - nearly $10 \times$ larger than $CeO_{2}$ ( 6 atoms), making every calculation far more expensive
