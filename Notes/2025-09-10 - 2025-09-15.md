## Converge the Brillouin-Zone Sampling (k-mesh)

- For your $\sim 10 \times 10 \times 9.6 Å$ cell, a $2 \times 2 \times 2$ Monkhorst-Pack is usually enough for precise statics; for MD you can often run $\Gamma$-only if you validated that the $2 \times 2 \times 2$ change in energy/forces is negligible. 
- Do a convergence test by changing the KSPACING parameter. KSPACING is suggested because it's reproducible as you change cells: try KSPACING $=0.25$ (tight statics) and 0.35-0.5 (MD), with KGAMMA = .TRUE. .


## Stabilize the SCF/Mixer Charge sloshing is largely a mixing problem on top of f-electron localization. 

For the "pre-convergence" static runs: 
- Use `ALGO = Normal` (or All if still twitchy). Avoid Fast until it's rock-solid. 
- Tighten `EDIFF = 1E-5` (statics). Keep `NELM = 150-200`, `NELMIN = 5` . 
- Start from atomic densities once: `ICHARG = 2` for the first static only; afterwards use the converged CHGCAR/WAVECAR. 
- Dampen mixing: reduce the linear mixing and enable Kerker preconditioning. A good starting point is smaller `AMIX` than default and nonzero `BMIX`; mirror the magnetic channels with `AMIX_MAG` / `BMIX_MAG` on the same order. (Exact numbers are system-dependent; the lever is: smaller `AMIX` = more stable; nonzero `BMIX` = suppresses long-wavelength sloshing.) 
- Optional but helpful: `NELMDL = -10` (no mixing for the first 10 electronic steps) to settle the subspace; `LDAUPRINT = 1` to inspect f-occupancies and site moments. If you see oscillations, temporarily increase smearing for the pre-convergence (e.g., ISMEAR = 1, SIGMA $=0.2$ ) just to get a clean starting wavefunction, then switch back to your production smearing.
