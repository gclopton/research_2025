# Phase 2: Equation-level Cylindrical TTM/i-TS Benchmarks

**Important dependency:** All Phase 2 benchmarks use material-specific TTM parameters ($g$, $\kappa_e$, $C_e$, $C_l$). The papers below provide these for metals, SiO2, and LiF. ### Gaps

- **No systematic SHI track cell-size convergence study exists.** Neither a universal minimum radial padding nor a systematic convergence test (varying cell size and measuring track-observable sensitivity) was found in the literature. The Gorbunov (2022) paper provides methodology for PBC artifact analysis, but you will need to perform your own convergence tests for your specific CeO2 system and ion/energy.
- **No timestep convergence protocol specific to SHI TTM-MD was found.** Practical guidance must be assembled from Darkins & Duffy (2018) general recommendations, the VASP/LAMMPS documentation, and your own convergence testing. The key constraint is that the first ~1 ps requires smaller dt than the annealing tail.

# Phase 6: MLIP Credibility Under Extreme Nonequilibrium Dynamics (Training Corpus Philosophy)

Phase 6 resources justify a training-corpus philosophy that remains stable under SHI-like extremes: multiple stoichiometries/phases, defects + strain, and high-temperature disordered states. The strongest precedents fall into two families: (i) local, scalable MLIPs that have survived radiation cascades and spike-like melt–quench excursions, and (ii) charge-aware MLIPs that explicitly represent long-range electrostatics and charge redistribution (e.g., QEq-like schemes or separated Coulomb terms).

# Phase 6D: MLIP Deployment in Production LAMMPS SHI Simulations

Phase 6D covers the handoff from a trained MLIP to a working SHI track simulation in LAMMPS. This is one of the highest-risk parts of the pipeline and the least documented in the literature. Topics: registering an MLIP pair_style with `fix ttm/grid`, splicing ZBL or other short-range repulsion for the close-encounter regime in the track core, extrapolation/out-of-distribution (OOD) detection during production runs, energy drift and stability under thermostatted nonequilibrium conditions, and performance tradeoffs (MLIP evaluation is slower than classical potentials, so cell size, timestep, and grid resolution decisions all change).
