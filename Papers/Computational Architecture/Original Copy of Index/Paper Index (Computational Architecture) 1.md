# Phase 4: Protocol Discipline, Artifacts, and Representativeness

Phase 4 resources are “methods transparency” anchors: papers that explicitly admit protocol choices can change the answer, and then show how they controlled, diagnosed, or parameterized those effects. The main failure modes Phase 4 targets are sink/grid placement artifacts, track-radius inference ambiguity, bulk vs surface geometry mismatches, and overlap/fluence representativeness.

# Phase 6: MLIP Credibility Under Extreme Nonequilibrium Dynamics (Training Corpus Philosophy)

Phase 6 resources justify a training-corpus philosophy that remains stable under SHI-like extremes: multiple stoichiometries/phases, defects + strain, and high-temperature disordered states. The strongest precedents fall into two families: (i) local, scalable MLIPs that have survived radiation cascades and spike-like melt–quench excursions, and (ii) charge-aware MLIPs that explicitly represent long-range electrostatics and charge redistribution (e.g., QEq-like schemes or separated Coulomb terms).
