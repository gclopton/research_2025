


# Paper Index: DFT+U Reference Data & MLIP Training

This index covers the *ab initio reference data* side of the ceria SHI project: how to generate trustworthy DFT+U labels for MLIP training, how to design and audit the training corpus, and how to validate the trained potential against redox-relevant benchmarks. It begins where "I need DFT+U data" starts and ends where "I have a trained MLIP ready to plug into LAMMPS" begins.

**When to reach for this index:** You are deciding VASP settings, debugging SCF convergence, designing AIMD sampling, auditing branch consistency, choosing an MLIP architecture, or validating defect/vacancy energetics against DFT+U references.

### Gaps

- **No canonical "AIMD branch-switch detector" paper exists.** The literature provides the ingredients for a diagnostic workflow (Allen 2014 for occupation-matrix control, VASP LDAUPRINT for outputting matrices, Meredig 2010 for navigating minima, Dorado 2009 for understanding metastable states), but no single paper packages this into a practical "monitor your DFT+U AIMD trajectory for branch switches" protocol. You will likely need to assemble this workflow yourself from these ingredients.
- **No dedicated "AIMD best practices for DFT+U oxides" paper exists.** The VASP documentation pages are the most directly applicable reference for oxide AIMD practice. The Leung et al. (2009) J. Chem. Phys. paper (DOI: 10.1063/1.3137054) is a useful DFT+U AIMD case study but is for ion hydration, not bulk oxides.
