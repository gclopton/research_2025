


# Paper Index: Ceria Radiation Chemistry & Hypothesis Anchors

This index collects the physical science and experimental foundation for the hypothesis that ceria's redox chemistry drives SHI track annealing and radiation tolerance. It covers experimental track morphology, irradiation-induced reduction, defect chemistry and oxygen transport, comparative radiation response across fluorite oxides, and theoretical frameworks for coupled redox-thermal models.

**When to reach for this index:** You are writing an introduction or motivation, designing the "redox on vs off" comparison, interpreting why the MLIP gives different track outcomes than the classical potential, or building the theoretical argument for a redox order parameter in track models.

## Section 7: Ceria Materials Properties That Differentiate the Redox Hypothesis from Alternatives

Papers here serve the hypothesis narrative: if a property (e.g., thermal conductivity, e-ph coupling) differs between the two materials, you need to know by how much, to assess whether your redox mechanism is needed on top of those differences or whether it is the dominant effect. Properties that are identical between CeO2 and UO2 strengthen the redox argument by elimination; properties that differ are confounders you must address.

### Gaps

- **Electron-phonon coupling constant (g) for CeO2:** No paper was found that reports a clean, metal-style scalar electron-phonon coupling constant for ceria suitable for direct TTM use. The Pelli Cresi et al. (2020) ultrafast paper gives the physics of electron-lattice energy transfer in CeO2, and Daraszewicz & Duffy (2011) and Medvedev et al. (2023) provide frameworks for extending TTM parameters to nonmetals, but extracting a CeO2-specific g value from these requires modeling work — it is not tabulated anywhere. This is a genuine parameter gap.
- **Electronic heat capacity C_e(T_e) for CeO2:** No paper was found that tabulates or parametrizes C_e as a function of electronic temperature for CeO2. For a wide-bandgap insulator with 4f states, this quantity must be constructed from the electronic density of states rather than taken from a free-electron model. The ceria DOS/electronic-structure papers (Da Silva 2007, Jiang 2005 in the DFT+U index) provide the input needed to build such a model, but the model itself has not been published for ceria.
- **Experimental Ce2O3 elastic constants:** No clearly verified experimental Ce2O3 elastic-constant paper was found. The Ce2O3 elastic data is DFT-only (Niu 2013, Qi 2013). This is acceptable for MLIP validation but means there is no independent experimental check for Ce2O3 mechanical response.
- **Direct experimental κ(T,δ) map for CeO2−x:** No single paper provides a broad, direct experimental thermal-conductivity map over both temperature and oxygen deficiency for pure CeO2−x. The Watanabe (2022) assessment is the closest, but a clean experimental dataset spanning the full (T, x) space does not appear to exist.
